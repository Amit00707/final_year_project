The Architect of Forgotten Dreams

Dr. Alistair Thorne

Review Score 4.9 / 5.0
Published Autumn 1924

In the silent corridors of the mind, memories are more than just echoes—they are structures built with the precision of a master builder. Dr. Thorne explores the intersection of subconscious architecture and the physics of the ethereal, weaving a narrative that challenges the very nature of what we consider "lost."

"A seminal work for those who walk the line between waking life and the library of the soul." — The Athenaeum Review
