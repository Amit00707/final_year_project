Book II • Chapter 1

Begin each day by telling yourself:

Today I shall be meeting with interference, ingratitude, insolence, disloyalty, ill-will, and selfishness – all of them due to the offenders' ignorance of what is good or evil. But for my part I have long perceived the nature of good and its nobility, the nature of evil and its meanness, and also the nature of the culprit himself, who is my brother (not in the physical sense, but as a fellow creature possessing a share of the Divine principle and of inheritance from the Deity).

Therefore none of those things can injure me, for nobody can implicate me in what is degrading. Neither can I be angry with my brother or fall out with him; for he and I were born to work together, like a man's two hands, feet or eyelids, or the upper and lower rows of his teeth. To obstruct each other is against Nature's law – and what is irritation or aversion but a form of obstruction.

Whatever this is that I am, it is a little flesh and breath, and the ruling part. Discard your books; no longer distract yourself with them: they are not your portion. Rather, as if on the point of death, despise this flesh; it is only a mixture of blood and bones, a network of nerves and veins and arteries. Consider what the breath is also: wind, and never the same, but every moment being puffed out and drawn in again.

Thirdly, then, comes the ruling part. On this foundation build your thoughts. You are an old man; say to yourself, "No longer allow this part of you to be a slave, no longer to be pulled this way and that like a puppet by every selfish impulse, no longer to fret at your present lot, or shrink from what the future holds in store."

The works of the gods are full of providence; even the works of fortune are not separate from nature, or without an interweaving and involution with the things which are governed by providence. Everything flows from that source; and there is besides necessity, and the interest of the whole universe, of which you are a part.
